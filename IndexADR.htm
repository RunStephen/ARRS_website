The Analytical Distance Runner

The Analytical Distance Runner focuses on races, 3000m and longer, including road, track, and crosscountry events. Male runners who have achieved a sub-30:00 time for 10K road or the equivalent, and female runners who have achieved a sub-35:00 time for 10K road or the equivalent are tracked. These criteria include virtually all world and national class open runners and many top masters runners.

Subscribe to the ANALYTICAL DISTANCE RUNNER (by e-mail only). ADR is distributed on a more or less weekly basis, depending on race results received and business trips. ADR highlights recent changes and updates to this website as well as providing competitive rankings for 25 countries, national team rankings, prize money rankings, statistical analyses, trivia questions, and more.

Special requests for customized statistical information should be addressed to kcy@inreach.com. Detailed runner bios, win/loss matrices, race handicapping, up-to-the-minute annual event lists, and many more are available at reasonable costs.

Have laptop, will travel. The ADR database resides on a laptop, making it possible to provide on-site statistical support on race day.

Race handicapping is also available for more than 100 races each year (men's and women's races counted separately). These tend to be major races and marathons involving five or (usually) more elite runners. The actual races handicapped depends on the timely receipt of elite entry rosters. An estimated time range and odds of winning are calculated for each runner. The three or four top picks are discussed in some depth. Post-race analyses of the handicapping are also provided.

NOTES

Road performances are classified as "record quality", "statistically valid", or "statistically invalid" based on the start/finish separation and the net drop of the course.

Record Quality
.....Start/finish separation is less than 30% of the race distance and the net drop is less than 1.0 m/km

Statistically Valid (suffix "a")
.....The net drop is less than 5.0 m/km and the net effect of tailwind and/or drop does not exceed 5 sec/km

Statistically Invalid (suffix "x")
.....The net drop is 5.0 m/km or more
.....The course has been shown to have been short on remeasurement
.....The course is suspect based on an analysis of the race time bias that exceeds 5 sec/km faster than expected (-5.0 sec/km)

Only record quality or statistically valid performances are included in records/rankings lists (except for the rate time bias adjusted lists that compensate for drop, shortness, and wind-aid).
