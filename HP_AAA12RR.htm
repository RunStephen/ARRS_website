Note: 1967 was the inaugural year.  The race was held at Leicester for 1967-1969 and at Derby for 1973.  For all years, the relay has consisted of six long legs and six short legs run in alternation, leading off with the long leg.

2016: the long legs were 5.38 miles (8.66 km)  and the short legs were 3.165 miles (5.09 km) for a total distance of 51.27 miles (82.51 km). 
2015: the long legs were 5.58 miles (8.98 km)  and the short legs were 3.165 miles (5.09 km) for a total distance of 52.47.62 miles (84.44 km). 
2014: the long legs were 5.08 miles (8.18 km)  and the short legs were 2.69 miles (4.33 km) for a total distance of 46.62 miles (75.03 km). 
1970-1972 and 1974-2013, the long legs were 5.38 miles (8.658 km) and the short legs were 2.995 miles (4.820 km) for a total distance of 80.87 km. 

For 1973 at Derby, the short leg was somewhat shorter than at Sutton Coldfield and the long leg was somewhat longer but the exact distances are not known.  At Leicester, the long leg was 5m1277y (9.21 km) and the short leg was 2m1160y (4.28 km) for 1969; the long leg was 5m1492y (9.41 km) and the short leg was 2m1502y (4.59 km) in 1968; and the long leg was 5m1600y (9.51 km) and the short leg was 3m705y (5.47 km) in 1967.  These distances for the short leg in 1967-1969 have been questioned and may have been longer than reported.
