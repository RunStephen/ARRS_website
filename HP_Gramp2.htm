Notes: 1877 was the inaugural year but the race distance was only one mile until 1886. Races for all years were handicapped with the handicap winner being listed; the third column gives the amount of the handicap (scr indicates a scratch runner, no handicap). Dates have been assumed to be the Monday following Easter Sunday. The 1887-1895 races were 15 minute runs with some distances not recorded (n/r). The 1886, 1896, and 1898-1899 race distance was 3 miles; the race distance for 1900-1941 and 1946-1972 was 2 miles. In 1935, A C Morgan was disqualified for incorrect performances, giving him too large of a handicap; the win was awarded to G Bayne (AUS). Times are given for the distance actually run which, in the case of races with a distance handicap, is the race distance minus the handicap distance.  In 2011, Margaret Wangari Muriuki (KEN) ran 8:49.54 off a 290 meter handicap.
